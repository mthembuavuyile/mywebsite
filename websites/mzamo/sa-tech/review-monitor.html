Best 144Hz Coding Monitors in South Africa Under R5,000

July 6, 2026 6 Minute Read SA Tech Team

As a software developer, your monitor is the single most important window into your work. While gamers chase high refresh rates for competitive frame rates, developers are beginning to discover that 120Hz and 144Hz refresh rates drastically reduce eye strain during long programming sessions.

Smooth scrolling through thousands of lines of code, seamless window transitions, and crisp rendering make high-refresh-rate IPS screens a game-changer. Historically, these monitors were expensive. Today, you can find stellar options under R5,000 on Amazon South Africa. Let's look at the absolute best performers right now.

Why High Refresh Rates Matter for Programmers

If you've only ever coded on standard 60Hz displays, you are missing out on a significant ergonomic upgrade. A 144Hz monitor refreshes the screen 144 times per second—more than double a standard panel. This yields several direct developer benefits:

  • Reduced Motion Blur: Scrolling text stays legible instead of turning into a smeared grey column.
  • Reduced Eye Strain: Fluid transitions minimize micro-adjustments in your focus, preventing fatigue.
  • Snappy UI Feedback: Moving windows and cursor tracking feel instantaneous.
"Once you switch from 60Hz to 144Hz, scrolling through documentation or terminal logs feels incredibly fluid. It is difficult to ever go back to standard refresh rates."

Ergonomic King: LG 24GN650-B UltraGear 24"

If you prefer coding in portrait mode for inspecting long single-file scripts, the LG 24GN650-B is your best option. Unlike the Gigabyte model, the LG's stand supports full vertical rotation (90 degrees). Combined with a gorgeous color profile, it remains a staple choice for modern developers.

How to Choose the Right Size

When selecting a monitor under R5,000, you will mostly be choosing between 24-inch and 27-inch screen sizes at 1080p resolution. Keep in mind that 1080p looks sharpest on a 24-inch panel. If you opt for 27-inch displays, the pixel density drops slightly, meaning you might want to increase text scaling inside VS Code to retain crispness.
