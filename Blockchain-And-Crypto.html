About Cryptocurrencies

Cryptocurrencies are digital forms of currency that use cryptography for security. Unlike traditional currencies controlled by governments, cryptocurrencies are decentralized and not controlled by a single entity. Transactions are typically recorded on a public ledger called a blockchain, which is shared across a network of computers.

  • Security through cryptography
  • Transparency via blockchain

"The Bitcoin Standard" by Saifedean Ammous

This influential book explores the history of money and argues that Bitcoin has the potential to become a new global standard, similar to the gold standard used in the past. It discusses the flaws of fiat currencies and the potential benefits of Bitcoin, such as its scarcity, divisibility, portability, and durability.
